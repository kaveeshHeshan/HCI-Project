M onkey P ose



Monkey pose is an advanced seated posture that increases flexibility in the hips, groin and legs - particularly the hamstrings and quadriceps.

In the fullest expression of this pose, the legs are in a front split position. One leg extends in front of the torso and the other extends behind the torso. The hand and arm positions can vary, but they are typically placed in prayer position or stretched upward to the sky.

Monkey pose may also be referred to by its Sanskrit name, Hanumanasana, which is derived from the name of the Hindu monkey god, Hanuman.



Benefits

  1. Hanumanasana Stretches and strengthen groin region, hamstrings and thigh muscles.
  2. It stimulates the digestive and reproductive organs, by boosting their functioning.
  3. Monkey Pose Makes hips flexible if practice daily.
  4. Stretches back muscles also.
  5. Helps to release tension and stress.
